Brief History:

Jefferson City was founded in 1821 and was named after the U.S. President Thomas Jefferson. It soon replaced St. Charles once the government buidling was completed. The city was laid out by Daniel Morgan Boone. It was officially incorporated in 1825 and in 1826 the state legislature moved there while the town was still small.
During those years Jefferson City population was 1,174, including 262 salves. A state penitentiary was built in 1836, which gave the city a positive strength. In 1837, the Capitol was burnt down and a few years later a new one was built.
In the 19th and early 20th centuries, Jefferson City has grown. Today, it has new infrastucture, hospitals, and transportation systems. A new Capitol was built in 1924. If you would like to know more about Jefferson City click here.

Population:

The current population is approximately 43,000 residents. Which serves as a smaller capital city compared to many other U.S. state capitals.

Region of the State:

The city is loacted in central Missouri, along the southern bank of the Missouri River. The river was the key reason why it was selected as the state capital.

City Classification:

Jefferson City is classified as a small urban city. The reason so is because it maintains a quiter atmosphere with suburban and rual characteristics in surrounding areas.
